Feature: User Preference Sync

Build a service that keeps user preferences synchronized across devices.

## Requirements

- Users can set preferences on any device and the changes should appear on all their other devices
- Preferences should sync in real time when the user is online
- Offline edits should be merged when connectivity is restored

## Open Questions

This description intentionally leaves several decisions unspecified that the specification
phase must surface and resolve before planning can proceed:

- What is the conflict resolution strategy when two devices edit the same preference key
  while offline simultaneously? (last-write-wins, server-authoritative, or manual merge?)
- What persistence layer backs the service? (relational DB, key-value store, or in-memory
  with a write-ahead log?)
- Are preferences scoped per-user globally, or per-user-per-application? The word
  "preferences" is used without defining the schema or namespace.
- What does "real time" mean in terms of latency SLO? (sub-second? eventual within 30s?)

The specification subagent should surface these as [NEEDS CLARIFICATION] items and
emit halt_directive=true, preventing the plan phase from running until the developer
resolves the ambiguities.
